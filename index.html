About Me

I am a 4th year SIAT student with a concentration in Game Design who has a keenness for building games on the foundation of uniqueness, detail, and visual appeal. With 4 dedicated years of hands-on experience, I have honed my skills in crafting immersive gaming experiences that resonate with players on both an emotional and intellectual level.


My journey in game design began at a young age when my leisure time was spent playing a variety of computer games on my dad's work computer. I was fascinated by the idea that a game so fun and immersive came from nothing but an idea in someone's head. This led me to pursue the industry of game design to better understand how great games are made. Now, my goal is to recreate my childhood fascination with games for the next generation.


I thrive on the collaborative nature of game development, relishing the opportunity to work alongside talented artists, programmers, and writers to bring ideas to life. Whether it's brainstorming game mechanics, fine-tuning level design, or shaping narrative arcs, I approach each aspect of game development with enthusiasm and a commitment to excellence.

Projects

A showcase of work from university projects and personal hobbies. My tool kit ranges from coding games in C# to painting on canvas with acrylic.


Slow & Steady

Unity | C# | Aseprite


An exhilarating, fast-paced top-down shooter where players take on the role of a CRT-TV to fight off rouge smart devices.


My role in this project was focused on the visual design. After agreeing on a pixel art style, I made the quick judegement to learn a new software called Aesprite. After building up a list of assets, I spent the second phase of the project integrating the art as well as programming the user interface.

Spirits of the Forest

Unity | C#


An immersive 2D side-scrolling platformer where a wise Wizard encounters fantastical puzzles, obstacles, and adventures.


Spirits of the Forest was my first project as the lead game developer. This meant I was in charge of designing and coding core game mechanics using Unity and C#. Another role as developer was being involved with and managing the group in aspects of the game such as iterating on gameplay flow/logic, art direction, and was involved in the storyline.
